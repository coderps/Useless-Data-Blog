This time we were really jobless in our lives, let us say it was because of “quarantine” [smiley]. So, we randomly started googling weird stuffs like “average time a human takes for peeing” (lets draw a line there [winking smiley]). Now, let us proceed with our methods.


METHODS


Disclaimer: Our methods are very unreliable and do not account for multitasking


Just googled and did some “awesome” calculations to generalize the time for an average human lifespan [79 years].


For example, if a person takes about a minute in cutting (and peeling) an average size potato and eats about 1 potato per day in his/her lifetime (compensating for the time when you overeat those fries and wedges, and also for the time when someone else cooks for you); that would give us an average time of 1 minute/day. Then this number is simply taken as a fraction to generalize over 79 years of a human lifespan to give a result of 20 days of our lives doing this particular activity. That’s a whopping 28835 potatoes in your lifetime! Which also roughly estimates to 0.07% of your lifespan. You could have easily utilized this time in watching the entire Star Wars series (including the two side ones) about 20 times.


I told you, I did some awesome calculations 😎
